    Feed the Kraken

    Overview

    This is a hidden role game with three teams. Team membership will be determined randomly, and number of players on each team differs by player countr

    • Sailors: who want to reach to the safety of bluewater bay. If the ship moves to one of the three blue spaces, they win the game
    • Pirates: who want to sabotage the mission and bring the ship to the crimson cove. If the ship moves to one of the three red spaces, they win the game
    • Cult leader: who wants to sacrifice themselves to the kraken. If the ship moves to the yellow space, they win the game

    There are a couple yellow kraken tokens on the board, when the ship moves into one of these spaces, the captain must...

    FEED THE KRAKEN (that's the name of the game) by sacrificing a single player. If they sacrifice the cult leader, then the cult leader wins the game

    Flowchart to link: https://boardgamegeek.com/filepage/238599/feed-the-kraken-flowchart

    Setup

    We will pass out tokens with our teams on them to everyone playing. Everyone will view their token in secret.

    Before starting, the pirates will know who each other are:

    • Everyone will close their eyes
    • We'll have the pirates open their eyes and gaze longingly at each other.
    • Then the pirates will close their eyes again.
    • Then everyone will open their eyes and we'll begin the game

    We'll also all get character cards that have one time use powers. There is a captain card mixed in to what is dealt out. That player will be the first captain and then draws a new character card.

    Round structure

    Captain assigns jobs

    Before we move across the map, the captain must assign two jobs. The navigator and lieutenant. Captain can't pick themselves or off duty players. Different players must be picked for each role

    This process happens in 3 steps (there is a reminder on the back of the captain's logbook)

    • Captain assigns roles
    • Crew proves loyalty or there is a mutiny
    • If mutiny happens, go back to step one with a new captain, other wise proceed to navigation

    Loyalty check

    • Crew can hide a number of pistols in their hand
      • If you are loyal to the captain, don't put any guns in your hand
      • If you are suspicious that the captain may be a pirate, you can try to mutiny by hiding pistols in your hand
    • Everyone holds their fist out over the table
    • When everyone is ready captain counts down and everyone reveals what is in their hand
      • Crew card shows how many total pistols are needed for a successful mutiny
      • If there is a successful mutiny, the player showing the most guns will be the next captain
        • Ties are resolved by the captain selecting one player who will NOT be the new captaion
        • If still tied, the player selected by the captain to not win will select another player to not win
        • This process is repeated until only one of the tied players remians, they are the new captain
      • The new captain gets the logbook, the navigator badge, and the lieutenant badge
    • This process repeats until the crew proves their loyalty and no mutiny happens

    After a successful mutiny, all guns players used in it are lost. Number of guns available to a player is public information outisde of the mutiny

    Once there is a successfuly vote, we move on to navigation

    Navigation

    4 steps that are also on the back of the logbook. All cards are drawn in secret.

    • Players selecting cards must remain silent and got give any signs or signals
    • Captain draws 2 navigation cards, chooses 1, and puts it face down in the log book. The other tile is discarded
    • The lieutenant draws 2 navigation cards, chooses 1 and puts it face down in the log book. The other is discarded. Make sure the cards are placed left and right.
    • Navigator gets the box, shakes it around, then looks at the cards inside. They discard one card.
    • The captain reveals the last card in the logbook and then executes it
    • Only after the captain has revealed the final selection can players talk about the options presented to them

    The revealed card dictates which arrow the ship will follow to move to the next hex on the board

    The revealed card is left in front of the captain in the area called "the captain's résumé. These cards will never get shuffled back in the deck.

    Any map action on the new spot will then be triggered, followed by any action on the navigation card

    • Map actions

      • Cabin search (magnifying glass): The captain secretly inspects another player's bag to find out their alliance. They can talk about the (alleged) findings
      • Off with the tongue: The captain gives a player the "off with their tongue" token. The selected player can no longer speak words, but can make gestures and and sounds. That player can not become captain. They still participate in mutinies, but in the event of a tie are considered to have 0 guns
      • Flogging: creates public information for all players
        • The captain picks a player to flog and hands them the 3 flogging cards
        • Player shuffles the cards face down, looks at them, selects the one that represents their team, and places it face down
        • The captain shuffles the other two cards, places them facedown on the table, and then picks one to reveal
        • The revealed card indicates a faction that the flogged player does not belong to. They put the "I AM NOT A" card and the selected faction card face up in front of the player as a reminder for the rest of the game
      • Feed the kraken
        • trhe captain chooses another player and feeds them to the kraken. That player is eliminated from the game, can't speak anymore, or reveal their faction. If the player fed to the kraken is the cult leader, that team wins

    • Navigation card effects (printed on inside of captains logbook)

      • Drunk: Captain looses position. The captain role moves clockwise to the player who have the fewest cards in their résumé (navigation cards in front of them)
      • Mermaid: Captain chooses anotehr player. That player shuffles the last 3 discareded navigation cards and secretly looks that them. They can discuss their (alleged) findings
      • Telescope: The captain chooses a player to look at the top card of the navigation draw pile. That player may leave it there or discard it
      • Armed: The navigateor gets 1 gun from the supply
      • Disarmed: The navigator must discard 1 gun from their supply
      • Cult Uprising: A cult ritual card is carried out at the end of the navigation

    Off duty

    After navigation refer to the off duty table

    • The indicated players involved in the current navigation get the off duty signs
    • The players who previously held the off duty signs are now available for selection in the next navigation phase
    • Off duty signs only change hands after a successful navigation, not a mutiny
    • Off duty players cannot be chosen as navigator or lieutenant, but can participate in a mutiny and can become captain

    End of a round

    • After navigation , the captain starts the next round by appointing the navigation team
    • If there are fewer than 4 cards in the deck before navigation shuffle and make a new deck before any cards are drawn
    • Well keep going through rounds until a game end condition is met
