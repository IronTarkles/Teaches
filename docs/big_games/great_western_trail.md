## Overview

We're ranchers in the west who want to make their fortunes selling cattle to the east. This game has deck building and hand management, where everything is in service of working your way along the trail to go sell cattle at market at the top of the board and ship them off to different cities. We'll also utilize different types of workers to construct buildings, buy new cows, and move our train around more.

There is no victory point track, all points are awarded at the end of the game. 

## Turn structure:

On your turn you'll do 3 things. Move, take actions in the space you land in, and then draw up to your handsize. There are spaces on your player hard to remind you of how these work

### Movement:

- Your base movement is listed on your board (4 in a 4p game), but you can increase that via upgrades. 
- You must always move forward at least 1 space, and you can never go backwards. 
- It takes one movement point per location you move across. Locations are tiles, any empty building site does not require movement to cross. 
- You can share spaces, and Kansas City (at the end of the trail) stops all movement.
- The space where you end up is the one you activate during the next part of your turn

Green and black hands: 

- These are shown on tiles you move over and indicate fees that you have to pay. 
- Green is one dollar, black is two.
- Hazard (point to hazard tiles) or outlaw tiles (point to outlaws) get paid to the bank, player tile fees get paid to that player.
- Not being able to pay does not stop your movement. Pay what you can, if anything, and keep moving. Never have to pay retroactivly. So if you land on a space you can't pay for but then earn money, you don't have to then pay the fee you missed.

### Activate tile actions

- If it's a neutral building or owned by you, activate either all location actions (actions on tile), or one auxillary action.
- The symbol for a single auxilliary action is a single gear, a double auxilliary action symbol is a double gear.
- Auxilliary actions are listed on the side of your playerboard, but we'll come back to those.
- Local actions can be done in any order, you don't have to do them all. Some have a cost, some have a slash for either/or, most have 1-3 options
- Many action spaces will cost a card or cards from your hand to do something on the tile. The tile will show a color of cattle that you must discard from your hand to gain the benefit show. No cattle means no action.
- If you land on a hazard, outlaw, or tile owned by another player, you can only take a single auxillary action.
- So you want to try and stop on neutral buildings or ones you own to get the most out of your turn

### Draw up to hand size

- At the end of your turn you will draw up to your max hand size
- Starts at 4, but can be upgraded

### Kansas City

There is one space you can end your turn on that we haven't talked about, Kansas City. This is where you send your cattle to market, make money, and return to the start of the trail. The reminder steps for this are on the board near the Kansas city stop

When you reach Kansas City, you must carry out the 5 steps in order

- Phase 1-3: Foresights: pick one of the two tiles and place it in the appropriate section. 
    - Hazards and outlaws go in the next lowest numbered space in the appropriate area. 
    - Workers get placed in their area following a few rules. Must be placed in the row with the job market token left to right. When the job market token would be covered move it down. 
    - This is also the game timer, when it reaches the end, the end of the game is triggered. Additionally when it reaches the halfway point, the cattle market is refilled.
- Phase 4: income:
    - Reveal your hand
    - Calculate your total breeding value by adding up the values from every unique cow in your hand. 
    - Duplicates will not be counted. 
    - You can increase your breeding value by using certificates:
    - (point to the certificates on your player board)
    - There are temporary certificates that you have a tracker for on your board, you can use them by moving the cube down or permanent ones that you can unlock
    - Once your breeding value is set, grab that much money and then discard your hand
- Phase 5: Deliver cattle to a city
    - You must now pick a city to deliver to based on your total breeding value (equal or lower)
    - Can't deliver to a city with your disk on it, with the exception of Kansas City and New York. 
    - Once you pick a city, select a disk from your board to place. This is how you can upgrade things like your board movement, hand size, certificate limit, and auxilliary actions.
    - Keep in mind the color space you take the disk from must match the color City you deliver to (black or white). 
    - You will then gain any bonuses shown on the city, and may gain or lose points at the end of the game 
    - Bonuses in green arrows aren't triggered unless you have a disk in both locations
    - Lastly, check to see if you need to pay transport costs. If your train is directly at the city or further up the track, it's free. If your train is further back, pay 1 for each crossing symbol between you and the destination city
- Move your herder to the start of the trail horse space
- Refill the forsight spaces from the appropriate bags

## Actions

We'll talk about some of the actions that are on the starting tiles, and that will touch upon some of the other major concepts of the game

### Hiring workers

Whenever you do this action you will hire a worker from the job market and move them to your player board.

- You can't hire a worker from the row with the job marker, only rows above
- The cost to hire has a base price of the row you're taking the worker from
- The action to get your worker can have a price modifier, red being an additional cost, green being a discount
- The worker is placed in the matching row on your playerboard
- Placing a worker may grant you a bonus or additional action
- You may not hire a worker if the row is full already
- Workers on the final 2 spaces of each track are worth end game points

### Buying cattle 

You starting cows aren't great, there is a market where you can buy better cows that are all also worth end game points

- The number of cowboys that you have dictates what options you can take with this action
- All of the available actions are shown at the bottom of the board in the cattle market
- Cowboys are "spent" whenever you perform cattle market actions (but not removed from your board). You can only use each cowboy once
- So with 2 cowboys, you could use a single 2 cowboy action, or you could perform 2 single cowboy actions
- You can always buy fewer cows than you are able
- Any cow you buy goes to your discard pile
- The cattle market will not refill until the halfway point of the game
- You can use a single cowboy to add 2 cards from the cattle market deck and add them to the lineup

#### Simmental variant

Adds a new color cow, orange!

- These have a market action as well, and will upgrade whenever they're in your hand when you go to Kansas City
- Start worth little breeding value and points as a level one cow
- Can upgrade to level 2 and then level 3, worth more each time

### Moving your train

You have to move your train down the line to deliver to bigger cities without having to pay out the ass

- The number of engineers you have dictates how far you can move your train
- Some action spaces show a symbol for 1 train movement for each engineer you have
- Some action spaces just show fixed train movement
- Train movement is always UP TO the value
- Each space on the train track can only hold one train. You skip over other trains ahead of you when counting movement
- There are a number of turnout spaces where you can veer off the main track to a train station
- When choosing to move a fewer number of train spaces, you lose you extra movement. So you can't stop a train station, do a thing, and then continue moving along

Upgrading a train station

- The reason you may want to veer off the main track is to upgrade a train station
- You can only upgrade a train station if you can pay the upgrade cost shown to the right of the space and don't already have a disk there
- This is the only other way to get disks off of your player board to upgrade things
- There is also a station master tile. You can get this tile by appointing one of your workers as the station master.
- You remove the worker from your board and swap them with the station master tile. This means only one player can get each tile
- You only have the option to do this the turn you visit a train station. You can't do it on a later turn.
- There are train stations that only allow you to upgarde and drop a disk, but don't have a tile
- The last space on the train track is special. If your movement ends there you can upgrade or not, but then you gain 3 dollars and move your train to any space on the train track that you want. If you choose to move it to a turnout space, you can immediately upgrade that space like normal
