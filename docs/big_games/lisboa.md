## Overview

In 1755 Lisboa (thats in Portugal) suffered an earthquake of 8.5-9 magnitude, followed by an earthquake, and then 3 days of fires. The city was almost completely destroyed. The game Lisboa is set over 22 years in which the surviving nobles (thats us) will help with the reconstruction and economic development of the new city to receive the graces of the king and the marquis.

### Board Overview

We'll need to clear rubble and build stores downtown (gesture towards grid where buildings will be built), we'll need to work with the architects to get plans for public buildings to build ( gesture at the blue and green architect piles) and make sure we have enough officials to staff the buildings. We'll need influence (gesture to influence track) to convince the nobles (gesture at the nobles) to help us, or we can also trade with them for state actions. We can meet with the clergy (gesture towards area with red meeple) to get clergy tiles which grant permanent bonuses, and we can also persuade the Marquis to make decrees that favor us, giving us more points at the end of the game (gesture at decree card lineup on the side of the board)

### Playerboard overview

- This is your portfolio (gesture at playerboard). Your warehouse on the left is where you hold your resources, and you start the game with one of each. 
- You then have a section where you store the rubble you collect in the game. The rubble cooresponds to the type of destruction that occured, red for fire damage, blue for tsunami damage, and tan for earthquake damage. 
- Collecting sets of rubble with both move along the pace of the game and dictate how many total resources you can hold as well as how many total cards you can play to your board. 
- Currently we can store 2 of each type of resource, and have a total of 2 cards played to our boards.
- Comleting a full set of rubble increases the capacity of each by 1
- In terms of playing cards to your board you have 3 wide slots up top, and 3 smaller slots at the bottom.
- Top part of the board is where noble cards and ships go, the bottom part of the board is where treasury cards go.
- To reiterate, your card capacity number refers to total cards in the top and bottom of your board.
- You also have a space to store your royal favors (under the rubble track)
- A space to store your building plans (to the right of favors), and we all start with one.
- Your buildings
- Your state officials (meeples)
- Your clergy tiles (top right). We all start with the choice of one of two dealt during setup.




### Game Structure

The game is played over 2 time periods. When a player manages to complete 2 sets of ruble or 3 decks of political cards are exhausted, the round ends at the end of that players turn. There will then be a mid game scoring. We'll then swap some stuff out on the board and start the 2nd time period until the end of the game is triggered by someone having 4 complete sets of rubble, or 3 decks of cards being exhuasted again. We will finish out the round, and then everyone wil lget one more turn before doing end game scoring

## Turn Structure

You will always have a hand of 5 political cards. Your turn consists of you playing a card in your hand to perform one of 4 actions or to get 1 gold. Then you will take a new card into your hand from the display and it will be the next players turn. The turn structure is detailed on the back of your player aid.

There are only 2 types of political cards. Nobles and treasury cards.

### Noble Cards

- Each noble card depicts one of the 3 nobles
- There is an amount of influence at the top of the card.
- The picture of the noble and flavor text in the middle
- A reward or penalty at the bottom of the card

### Treasury Cards

- Subsidy and Treasury modifyer up top
- Action or reward in the middle
- Flavor text
- Money or Sales benefit

### Playing a card

- On your turn you will select a card from your hand and decide whether to play it to your portfolio, or play it to the royal court to visit the nobles on the main board.
- Your turn structure is detailed on the back of your player aid as well as the section in the middle of your board.

#### Playing card to portfolio

- (Point to the top part of the left half of the turn summary on the playerboard)
- Decide to play a noble card to the top of your board, or a treasury card to the bottom
- Playing a noble card to the top earns you the bonus shown on the arrow at the bottom of the card, then you tuck that card in the top of your playerboard.
- Treasury cards are tucked in the bottom and earn you money equal to the current treasury value (point to the big cube on main board), and then decrease the treasury marker
- Treasury also have an ongoing effect shown at the bottom that remains visible when tucking the card.

You then decide whether to sell goods or trade with a noble (point to the bottom part of the left half of the turn summary on the playerboard)

##### Selling a good (main action)

- To sell a good, someone at the table must have a ship built on the top part of their portfolio (grab a ship card and put it in the top of the portfolio to show)
- If there is a ship built and it has room for goods (point to the box and number on the ship card) you can sell to it.
- Select any docked ship with space and move a good from your warehouse under that ship into one of the circular spaces. This could mean that you are putting a good from your portfolio onto another players portfolio.
- Recieve money equal to the goods current price (gesture towards good price track), plus any modifier depicted on the ship.

If the number of goods on the dock spaces below the ship are now equal to the ship size, the ship immediately sets sail and the owner of the ship completes these steps

- Flip the goods tokens on the dock spaces below the ship face down so the crate side shows
- Earn 1 point per good packed up

You can sell any number of goods to any number of ships up to their capacity.

Your other choice instead of selling a good is to trade with a noble (point at handshake symbol on left half of turn reminder on player board)

##### Trading with a noble (main action)

This action allows you to take up to two different state actions that are associated with each noble on the main board. The state actions are shown in the little banners under each noble's portrait.

- Each noble is associated with 2 specific state actions, and each noble cares about one type of good.
- Trading with a noble allows you to take up to 2 actions, but each action will cost you a good.
- In the banner showing the actions, you can also see the good required to trade with that noble. You can also use gold with each noble instead.
- When you take an action, take the good or money from your portfolio and cover up the action on the main board in that noble's section.
- One that action is complete, you decide if you want to pay another resource to take the other available action under that noble.
- First action is mandatory, second is optional.

###### State actions

We will start with the builder's office (the green guy)

Recruiting state officials (two men symbol)

- Move up to two state officials, those are your meeples, from your playerboard to two different nobles offices. 
- If the office has no free space for your official, determine which color has the most meeples in that office and move one to the plaza below to free up space for your meeple
- In the event of a tie, move one of each tied color

Acquire a plan (the paper)

- Take the top plan from either architects stack (gesture to the blue and green builders on the right side of the board)
- Place it on the proper space on your player board, architect side up.
- You can have any number of plans from both architects

Next the Marquis office (the blue guy)

Build a ship (the boat)

- Pay a number of different goods equal to the hull size of the ship visible in the shipyard (point to the ship cards on the main board)
- You pay the difference in size if you are replacing a ship in your portfolio
- If you replace a ship, the cargo stays where it was (it is loaded on to the new ship)
- Select an empty slot at the top of your board or discard a card to make room for the ship
- Move the tresury marker up one spce
- Earn the influence shown at the top of all your tucked cards, including the new ship (point at the influence track) More on the influence track later

Produce goods (the gears)

- From each store you own, take one good of its business type (the street that it is facing) from the supply. More on that in a bit
- Reminder that your limit of goods you can hold is 2 of each plus 1 additional for each rubble set you've completed
- Each type of good produced drops 1 money in price, so the market marker for it moves accordingly (point at goods market price track)

Finally the Kings office (the pink one)

Meet the cardinal (cross with arrows)

- Advance the cardinal meeple clockwise one or two spaces. The spaces he moves are in between the clergy tiles.
- Take either one of the tiles now adjacent to the cardinal, placing it face up on your player board.
- You must have free space for this. Once you have 4 tiles, you cannot take this action.
- Check to see if the cardinal landed on or passed over an icon.
- Depending on symobs the cardinal has moved over, either increase the treasure marker 1 space, or perform a church scoring at the end of that players turn
- For church socring each player, starting with the player who moved the cardinal, decides whether to gain influence or not. 
- To gain influence, discard a clergy tile. Earn the points shown on the back. Gain influence shown on your tucked cards.

Get a royal favor (the crown)

- Each royal favor tile depicts on of the nobles and allows you to take an extra action in that nobles office during any other players turn
- You can only get a royal favor that you do not already own. 
- Grab a tile and put it in the appropriate spot on your board.

TO REITERATE, PLAYING A CARD TO YOUR BOARD ALLOWS YOU TO THEN EITHER SELL GOODS TO SHIPS, OR TAKE STATE ACTIONS WITH A SINGLE NOBLE

EACH OF THESE OPTIONS REQUIRES YOU TO PAY GOODS FROM YOUR STOREHOUSE, SO IF YOU DON'T HAVE GOODS (OR MONEY FOR NOBLES) THEN YOU CAN'T DO THESE OPTIONS

#### Playing card to the royal court

- Instead of playing a card to your board, you can play to the court (gesture to the top part of the right side of the playerboard turn reminder)
- If its a noble card, place it at the top of the nobles area and put your courier meeple on top of it
- This is the noble you will visit, and this is where in influence track comes in
- To meet with the noble, you must pay influence equal to the number of officials in his office that don't belong to you
- There is a cost modifer to this action based on the position of the treasury cube as well. It can be cheaper or more expensive
- You can then optionally take one of that noble's state actions first (still paying the resource)
- You must then take the noble's main action (shown to the right of the state actions)

##### Green action: Build a store

- The stores will go in the downtown area and will have the door (the triangle cut out) pointing towards a color street.
- Grab a store from the line up on the side of the board next to the downtown area.
- The street your store is facing is the type of good that it will produce
- Gain the reward depicted on the land space
- Take one rubble from the column or row where that building is built
- Pay for the land:
    - Pay the current treasury value
    - Pay for each piece of rubble on in the row and column where the store was built
    - Rubble in public building spots is NOT considered when paying
    - Cost of rubble is shown below the city chart
- Choose a wooden house:
    - From your board, grab a house from one of the three house tracks.
    - You must grab the lowest from one of the tracks
    - There are passive powers that unlock once the house next to the icon has been removed
    - The leftmost allows you to spend coins instead of influence at a 1:1 rate
    - The middle allows you to get a discount of a good for a ship, or for ships to be totally free
    - The right gives you a single additonal good of a type you produced, then gives you an additional good of EACH type when you produce
    - These bonuses are upgrades to each other, they are not cumulative
- Score points:
    - Check to see the number of relevant public buildings to the store that you just built
    - There can be a max of three that match it. Look to see if there is a color match in the row or on either side of the column
    - You then score points equal to the number shown at the bottom of the row on the point tile times the number of relevant public buildings
    - This means that a store with no relevant public buildings will score no points
    - Buildings can score on later turns once relevant public buildings are built. This means that a store could end up scoring multiple times.

Stores will not refill to the side of the board until the end of your turn.

##### Blue action: Take a decree

Decrees earn you points at the end of the game. When you take this action, grab a decree from the display and put it face up next to your board.

When you make a set of ruble tokens, you remove a rubble set token from your board and put it on the blue noble's face area.

Whenever you take the decree action, you can remove one of your rubble set tokens from this area to then immediately grab a second decree.

Decrees don't refill until the end of your turn.

##### Pink action: Open a public building

Public buildings determine which stores will score, though they do not gain any points themselves. To open a public building:

- Select a construction site at the west, north, or east side of downtown
    - The north side buildings must have a color that matches the street on which it sits
    - West and East side buildings have no such restrictions
- Take both rubble cubes from the space, put them on your playerboard, and receive the reward from the space
- Reveal an incomplete plan tile matching the color of the architect on the public building you want to build
- Return a number of state officials depicted on the tile form nobles offices or the plaza to your player board.
    - If you don't have enough, you can hire officials by paying the current treasury value per official needed
- Flip the plan tile used and place it on the completed plan space on your board.
- Score stores
     - For east or west public buildings, the owner of each store in the same row earns points if the store faces a street of any of the colors on the public building
     - For north public buildings the owner of each store facing its street earns points
     - In both cases the number of points awarded in the number on the scoring tile at the bottom of the column
- Immediately replenish the publich building spot on the main board with the next tile

##### Following a noble card played to the board

If you have a favor tile matching the color card played to the board, you can spend it to take an action of the matching noble immediately after the current player finishes their turn.

- This is considered to still be on that player's turn, so things that don't refill until the end of their turn have not refilled yet (like decrees or constructable stores)
- You can choose either state action or the main action associated with the noble
- Whichever you choose, that is the only thing that you do (so you can't do 2 state actions like if you were trading with that noble).
- If you choose the state action you must pay the appropriate resource like normal
- To do either, you must first pay influence for each official in that office that is not yours

##### Playing a treasury card to the royal court

Playing a treasury card to the royal court lets you host an event.

- Pay the current treasury price
- Carry out what is shown on the main action of the card
- Your turn is over, players may not follow a treasury card like they could with a noble card.

### End of your turn

Take a card to replenish your hand. Replenish anything missing from the board. Reveal a new political card.

## End of a period

If during a player's turn, they complete a 2nd rubble set or 3 political card piles are empty, the round ends after that player's turn and a mid game scoring occurs:

- In turn order carry out these steps
- Players recieve 3 points for each of their own completed rubble sets
- Discard any ship cards in the remaining shipyard, replacing them with the brown ships with purple ships on top
- PLayers may discard any number of cards. Earn the reward on the bottom of one of each noble card discarded this way (ignore any penalties)
- Draw up to five cards from the purple deck. After everyone has drawn the purple deck goes back in the box
- Remove all red cards from the board and replace them with the brown cards
- Once this is all done, play resumes with the next player in turn order

## End of the game

If during a player's turn, they cmplete a 4th set of rubble cubles or 3 political card piles are empty, finish out the round. Then play one additional round.

End game scoring:

- Points equal to the hull size of all owned ships
- 3 points for each completed rubble set
- Points for having more stores of each type than opponents, according to the chart where the stores lie on the board
- Ties are broken by adding up the tied spots and dividing by tied players (rounding down) then awarding each those points
- Gain coins equal to what is below your influence marker, then earn 1 point for every 5 coins you have
- Count up the officials on your completed plans. First place gets 15, 2nd gets 10, 3rd gets 5
- 2 points for each royal favor left on your board
- Points for all of your decree cards
