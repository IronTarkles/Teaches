## Overview

We're teams of architects that are working to build St. Basil's cathedral.

This is a resource management rondel game where we're all contributing to the same shared set of buildings, but whoever contributes the most to each building will earn more points.

The game is a vaiable number of rounds, and the end of the game is triggered whenever someone builds their 6th  cathedral section

## Boards overview

- On the central board we'll be moving dice around to gain resources and take actions
- The cards to the side are the different buildings of the cathedral that we're all working to build

The score track on the board is made up of recognition points (cross symbol with the beige background) and prestige points (the eagle looking thing)

- Prestige (cross) is a direct gain or loss of that number of spaces
- Recognition (bird) you move your marker to the closest recognition space forward or backward
    - Starting out, the gap between regonition points is several prestige points
    - Later this becomes 1:1

## Turns overview

A turn consists of a player taking a single action, and then the next player taking their turn. We'll continue like this clockwise and turn order will never change.

The 3 actions are claiming a section of the cathedral, building a section of it, or getting resources to use to build

### Claim a cathedral card

This action reserves a section of the cathedral for you alone to build on. It also removes banners that can increase your inventory space.

- Take a banner from your personal board
- Put you banner on an available cathedral card to claim
    - A card is available if
        - There are no other banners on it
        - It is the base of a tower, or a card directly above a card with any player's banner on it
- Take the workshop tile on the claimed cathedral card and place it on any empty workshop space on your board and either:
    - Pay the cost in that workshop space and place the tile face up. You will now gain the bonus shown whenever you use the die of that color
    - Don't pay, and don't place the tile
    - You unlock the ornamentation from that slot as well, and move it to the central part of your playerboard where it is now available for use
- If the workshop tile is paid and placed, you immediately gain the benefit

### Building sections

This is how you construct your reserved sections. Each section of the cathedral shows the requirements to construct it at the top of the card

- Deliver up to 3 materials to cathedral sections
- Take the materials from your board and deliver to one or more cathedral cards that have your banner on them
- There is no requirement that you finish a build in a single delivery

A cathedral section is finished once all the required materials are on the card

- Immediately gain the points and or money shown on the card
- Flip the card over, but keep your banner on it

If a section of the cathedral is completed while there are incomplete sections below it, the players below will be penalized

- Each player loses points equal to the number of completed sections above it

#### Decorations

This is how to spice up sections of the cathedral with the things we're unlocking on our board

- Once a section of the cathedral is completed, it can be decorated BY ANY PLAYER
- This is done by doing a build action to deliver the required materials for the ornamentation
    - Unlike the normal build action, all the materials for a decoration must be sent in the same action, no splitting across turns
- These costs are shown in the central area of the board. There is a base cost that you can add gems to.
- Each gem will get you one prestige (eagle) point, and you can get 3 points total if both gems are different
- You get no points without gems, but the decoration still counts towards majority ownership of a tower, which is important for end game scoring

### Getting resources

- Announce which market die you will be using
- Move that die a number of spaces clockwise equal to the value of the die
    - If you're moving the white die, or the die of your player color, you may pay 1 moeny to move 1 extra space multiple times
    - A die can't end in a space with 3 die already there. You would have to choose a different die, or pay extra to move it more if the die were white or player colored
- The die will stay in the area you moved it to
- Perform any market actions based on the space
- Roll all of the dice in that space

##### Market actions

Each of these can be done once on a resource action turn

- Obtain resources
    - Take the indicated resource times the number of dice in that space. You can take less
    - Resources in the supply are piece limited
    - Put the items in your inventory. You only have a set number of slots
    - If you get resources but have no open space in your inventory, you cannot rearrange to make space
- Use influence
    - The quadrant of the board where the die ended movement dictates which card you activate
    - Choose one of the two actions on that card to use
    - Blue lighting bolt actions are one time use
    - Red infinite actions can be done as many times as you want
- Activate a workshop tile
    - If you have a workshop tile on the die space matching the die used this turn, gain that bonus
    - Although the white die can have two workshop tiles, you only get to activate one

## End of the game

The game is over whenever someone builds their 6th cathedral card. They get 3 additional prestige (eagle) points for triggering. Everyone else then gets one final turn.

## Scoring

- All players move their marker down to the next closest prestige (eagle) point. If they are already on one, don't move
- Add up all materials and money in supply, convert 5:1\
- Points for towers
    - The total value of a tower is 2 prestige (eagle) points for each section completed, plus 1 point for each ornamentation on it.
    - Incomplete sections are not scored.
    - Each player counts the number of banners and ornamentation of their color on completed cathedral cards in each tower
    - The player with the most gets full points for a tower, with 2nd place getting half of that (roudning down), third gets half of that, and so on.
