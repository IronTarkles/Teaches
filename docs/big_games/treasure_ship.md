## Overview

Ming dynasty, bitches. Zhang He sailed across 7 voyages to different contries over the course of 30 years. We take the role of offices in his fleet contributing to the seven grand voyages.

## Round Structure

The game is made up of 7 voyages, with each voyage being a round. Each round is made up of 4 phases, and during the voyage phase we will be repeating some stuff for each leg of the voyage. These phases are detailed on the "Game Flow" part of the player aide

1. Dignitary phase: Where we'll get some bonuses and determine turn order
2. Planning Phase: Where we'll determine who will make the decisions for each leg of the voyage
3. Voyage Phase: Where the fleet will move across the board, and players take their actions
4. End of round phase: Cleanup phase

There is a more detailed breakdown of the phases on the back side of the player aide

## Dignitary Phase

- The dignitary board has rows of cards that we can interact with for different bonuses. 
- There is a single space with the Ming emporer Yongle up top, he's the one that sent Zheng He out on the voyages
- The bottom tier of dignitaries at the bottom can be visted by each player multiple times per game. Dignitaries in the other tiers (and the emperor), can only be visited once per game by each player
- We can share spots on the dignitary cards, the first person just puts theirs furthest left, and later players go further right

During this phase, we will take turns putting favor tokens (the crates) onto a dignitary card, beginning with the first player.

- Place your favor token on the bottom portion of the card
- Pay any costs in the dark bar up top
- Gain the rewards shown in the white box in the center of the card

## Planning Phase

In this phase we will determine the fleet commander who will make the decisions for each leg of the voyage.

Turn order for this phase is determined by where dignitaries were placed in the last phase:

- Higher tier dignitary is earlier in turn order
- If placed in same tier, leftmost cards are considered earlier
- If placed on the same card, leftmost player is considered earlier

Following this order, we'll place a voyage priority token (the ship) onto the spaces on the planning board ( the board with the four colored ships and round tracker)

The planning board shows a round tracker in the top left that also indicates how many legs will be on each voyage. The first two voyages are 3 legs, with the raminder of them being 4 legs.

There will be a voyage card on each leg section for the voyage (red is first leg, blue is second, so on). So the first round, there will only be 3 voyage cards, and in a 4 player game, someone won't get to be a fleet commander for a leg.

In turn order we'll place a marker on a voyage card, and there can only be one marker per card.

Yes, this is done blind, but you will soon see why you may want to go sooner or later in the legs of the voyage.

## Voyage phase

This is where the fleet moves on the board and where player turns will occur. We'll be repeating this phase for each leg of the current voyage.

### Reveal voyage card

- Flip over the leftmost unrevealed voyage card, resolving an event if there is one.
- The player with their ship token on the card is the current fleet commander
    - They have the initiative for the leg, deicde the route, and will choose their action first
    - Other players will need an order token (purple scroll) to perform an action on the same space that the fleet commander selected

### Move the fleet

- Fleet commander takes tokens equal to the leg distance shown on the card. One taken must be the large one, this is the commander token.
- For the first leg, the space marked zero on the coast of china will be the starting point
- The commander will put their tokens on the next nubmered spaces. Again using the example of the first leg, the red tokens would be placed on spaces 1, 2, and 3.
- The commander decides which of the places traveled during this leg will get the larger command token, and which will get the smaller route tokens.
- The commander will end up taking the action where the command token is placed, with other places only taken actions at the places where route tokens were placed (unless spending a special token to take the commander's action instead).
- After the fleet commander has placed all the tokens, check the voyage card for a local event. The red circle indicates which stop on this leg will be affected by the event. (leftmost space on card is first stop, etc).
- Place the correct event token near that space as a reminder

Further legs

- From the second leg onward, the starting point will be where the final ship of the last leg was placed.
- The new commander will follow the route arrows on the board to place their tokens one at a time.
- If a space has multiple outgoing arrows, the commander decides which to follow.
- Some routes have arrows on both ends, but you can't backtrack on the same leg. You can backtrack on a further leg of the voyage.
- There is an exception for a couple spaces, 23 and 28. If you place on either of these and still have extra to spend, you can place them on any space connect by arrows to a place where ships were put this leg
    - Example: If the leg distance was 4, and you placed on 21, 22, and 23 you have 1 token left. You can place it on 20 or 24, because these spaces are connected to this legs tokens and have not yet been used.

### Actions

After the commander moves the fleet, players will all take actions.

- The fleet commander will perform the action on the space where they placed the commander token.
- Other places chose a space with a route token and perform the action there. They can choose the same action space. There is nothing done to indicate which space non commander players have chosesn.
- Normally, only the commander can take the action where the commander token is, but any player can spend a purple order token to do the action of the commander space instead of one of the route spaces.
- Players can perform their actions simulaneously

The actions of a space are performed in a specific order:

1. Pirate encounter (if they're present)
2. Player actions
3. Raising monuments
4. Sending representatives

#### Pirate encounter

If a player chooses a space with a pirate mark, or where an event spawned pirates they must either fight, bribe, or evade the pirates.

- Fight
    - Calculate pirates power by counting number of pirate symbols on the space, symbols added by event, and added by the round modifier
    - Player's military force is calculated: 1 per weapon token spent (the red swords) + Firing cannons
        - Cannon force is equal to the number of red cannon tokens. You have a cannon fire tile on your playerboard that can be flipped to fire the cannons (thus adding a number equal to that of your cannon tokens). The cannon can only be used once per voyage. So if you use it in the first leg, you can't use it in any other legs of the same voyage.
    - If you defeat the pirates, you will move your token up on the military rep track according to the pirates board value + event value (do not add round modifier)
    - You gain any bonuses you pass over or end on.
- Bribe
    - Pay a gift token (orange chest) to avoid a fight. Only costs 1 token regardless of pirate strength
- Evade
    - If you lack both weapons and gifts (or choose not to use them), you can just evade
    - This means you will skip performing player actions and raising monuments, but you will still send representatives.
    - You should probably avoid doing this, it sounds dumb.

#### Player actions

- Resolve the action space by gaining whatever symbol is shown on the board. If there is a pirate symbol, you only gain the space's reward if you defeated or bribed the pirates
- If there is an event that effects the space, you choose whether to resolve the action or event first
- If you have a monument token on the space, you will perform the action twice. You will not perform the event a second time.

Some spaces work a little different

- Merchant spaces (cloth bag with an arrow to a conversion chart): Trade one on a merchant, or twice if you have a monument token there. Pay whats on the left and get your choice of one thing to the right of the arrow
- Major countries (the thick tiles): On a major country space, do all of the following:
    - (Optionally) Send out an emissary: You can spend the depicted emisaries (discard them) to raise up a diplomacy level with a country. We start at level 0. When you pay the depicted number of that country's emissaries, you put one of your diplomacy tokens (the people) onto the country tile to keep track of your new level. Whenever you level up your rating with that country again, just move your token up. You can only level up once per major country action, but if you have a monument (or build one later on the same turn), you can potentially level up twice.
    - Receive gift: Gain all the bonuses shown on the contry tile's central column for your current level and lower. Items in parenthesis are first time bonuses that will only be obtained once. On further actions where you get rewards you would ignore the bonus in parentheses. Points in the rightmost column are awarded at the end of the game
    - You can recieve a gift even if you do not send out an emessairy. You will gain nothing if your diplomacy level with a country is level 0.
- Animal tiles:
    - Gained via action spaces with Roman numerals, military rep track advances, and raising diplomacy with the Timurid empire.
    - The giraffe on Aden and phoenix on Madagascar can only be gained by the fleet commander
    - When you gain an animal tile, you choose an option
        - Put it in the animal housing on your player board
            - The first tile placed must cover at least one space marked with "start"
            - Further tiles gained must be orthoganally adjacent to an existing tile
            - When you cover icons, gain the bonus
        - Gain VP instead of gaining tile
            - Immediately gain VP equal the the number of spaces of an animal tile. Gain any VP shown on the tile (giraffe and pheonix)
            - Use this option if your housing is full or the animal won't fit. It is worth less points than scoring the stuff in storage

#### Raising Monuments

- You can raise a monument only on a space where you just took an action and don't already have a monument.
- Pay the amount of gold shown on the monument area to add one of your monument tokens there
- Monuments let you perform the action in that space twice on further turns
- When you first build a monument, you immediately get a bonus effect
    - Take the action a second time (for a total of two this leg)
    - Perform the action of each connected space where you already have a monument
        - Local events aren't repeated
        - Order tokens don't have to be spent for spaces with the Commander token

#### Send out a representative

Pay 1 representative token (the square ship token) to take an extra action. 

- This can only be done once per leg, but multiple times in a voyage.
- Your choices of spaces to activate are ones with a route or commander token on them, or spaces adjacent to those tokens. 
- If you have a monument in the chose space, perform the action twice
- Restrictions:
    - You can't choose a space where you already acted this step.
    - Performing the action where the commander token is or where the no entry event is requires you to spend an order token
    - Ignore events besides no entry
    - You can't raise a monument in the space where you're taking this bonus action

### End of the round

When all legs of the voyage have been completed, we'll do some cleanup for the next round.

- Start player token moves to the left
- All favor tokens on dignitary cards are moved off of the card to the area on the right of them
- Take back all route and commander tokens from the map and return them to the voyage board.
- Remove all event tokens from the main board
- Remove the first leg's voyage card from the game
- Draw a single card from the extra voyage cards and add it with the cards used for the legs to the voyage deck. Shuffle and deal face down for the next voyage.
- Players refresh cannons
- adnace the round marker

## Scoring

We do on final diplomatic phase before moving to scoring. 

- Current VP
- VP for each dignitary card where you place a favor token. There are conditions for some of these
- Military repuation track. Gain the VP of the space your on or the nearest one below. You're only gaining the VP of a single space, it is not cumulative for everything you've passed
- Major countries:
    - Diplomacy level 1 or 2: Gain VP equal to your level
    - Diplomacy level 3: Gain VP equal to level 3 plus VP according to the number of emessairy tokens of that country that you hold
    - If level 3 is not reached your emissairies of that country don't score you anything.
- Animals
    - Gain VP equal to the total squares covered by animal tiles in your housing area
    - Then get 1 Vp for every space in your biggest rectangle covered
    - 1 VP per monument
    - 1 VP for every 2 trade goods left
    - 1 VP for every 3 gold left
    - 1 VP for every order, representative, and gift token left
