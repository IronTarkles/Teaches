## Overview

We're all the heads of noble families in Florence during the Renaissance. We're sending out our family members to collect resources, commisiosion buildings, increase military strength, influence people, support ventures, and of couse, appease the church.

The game is played over 3 periods, each lasting 2 rounds for a total of 6 rounds, after which the player with the most victory points is the winner

## Round structure

- We begin each round by rolling three die
- Each player has a family member equating to each die, they get the value of the corresponding die when placed on the board
- Each player also has a neutral member with a value of 0
- In turn order we'll send 1 family member out onto the board. That family member will perform an action using the value of its corresponding die.
- All of the action spaces show the minimum die value needed to send a family member there
- Action spaces can only contain one family member (spots are blocked for the round)
- Servants (purple meeples) can be paid to increase the value of a family member by 1 for each spent
- Servants will have to be paid to get neutral family members above 0

## Actions

### The towers

This is where you can acquire new cards. 

- Placement rules:
    - The family member you place must have a value equal to or exceeding the action space requirement
    - You can only have only family member per tower
    - If there are other family members in the tower where you're placing, you have to pay 3 money (total, not per)
    - Neutral family members are not considered to be any color
        - So you can have a neutral family member and a player color family member both in the same tower
        - There can also be any number of neutral colroed family members in the same tower
    - Pay for the card if necessary, then remove it from the tower. Will not be refilled this round.

When you get a new card that has a lightning bolt symbol, gain the bonus shown.

- Green cards
    - These get played on the bottom portion of your playerboard
    - Have no cost, but you must have the required military strength shown on the space where the card will go (the number in the shield). This is a check, they are not spent.
    - Military track is on the right side of the board
    - These cards will produce resources via another action
- Yellow Cards
    - These get played in the top slots of your playerboard
    - These cards cost resources
    - These cards will allow you to convert resources to other things via another action
- Blue cards
    - Played to the side of your bottom row of playerboard
    - Cost gold to play
    - Provide a variety of bonuses.
- Purple cards
    - Played to the side of your top row of playerboard
    - Cost resources or military points
    - Award end game scoring

### Harvest area (the axe)

This is how you activate your green cards on your playerboard

- Action space on the left (axe with requirement 1) may contain only 1 family member
- Action space on the right can contain any number of family members, but has a -3 modifier to the value. A family member placed here must have a value of at least one after appying the -3
- The entire harvest area, so the small action space + the large action space, cannot hold more than one family member of the same color (again, neutral family members get around this)
- You will activate all green cards with a die value equal to or lower than your harvest action value to gain all the bonuses shown at the bottom of the cards
- You will also activate the personal bonus listed by the action on your board (shows a die with a value of 1, so it will always activate)

### Production area (the gear)

This action works the exact same way as the harvest action, except for yellow cards. These cards will generally convert resources into things

- You must have all the items present when activating a card

### Market

4 action spaces above the dice that grant several different bonuses

- 5 coins
- 5 servants
- 3 military points + 2 coins
- 2 different council priveleges

![privleges](images/lorenzo/lim_priveleges.png)

A council privelege allows you to get your choice of bonus shown.

### The council

This action space (show in pic above at top) allows you to get on privelege and one coin.

The order in which the players use the council determines the turn order for the next round

## End of a round

After all players have used all their family members:

- Remove all remaining cards from the tower, then refill all 
- Reroll the dice
- Update turn order based on council action space (first to use action goes first, 2nd goes 2nd, so on)
- All players grab family members from board

## End of a period

At the end of every period of two rounds, so after rounds 2, 4, and 6, there is a church phase

- Check to see if your faith marker made it to the minimum required level on the faith track. There are 3 different outcomes
    - If you don't meet the required level, put a cube on the period token. You now suffer this penalty for the rest of the game. Your disk stays where it is
    - If you meet the requirement, decide to either:
        - Not support the church: put a cube on the period token. Suffer that penalty for the rest of the game. Leave the disk where it is (essentailly the same as failing the check)
        - Support the church: Gain the points shown on your step of the faith track. Do not place a cube. Reset your faith marker to the beginning of the faith track.

## End of the game

After the end of the third period (so the end of 6 rounds) proceed to scoring:

- Points of the highest space you have a green card in (reminder in space)
- Number of blue cards acquired (reminder at bottom of playerboard)
- Points from purple cards
- Points for first & second place on military track ( reminder at top of military track)
- Combine all resources, 5:1 conversion
