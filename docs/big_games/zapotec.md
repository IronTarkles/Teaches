## Overview

We're building temples, cornfields and villiages in the valleys surrounding the captial to generate the resources needed to build pyramids, make sacrifices to the gods, and perform rituals.

This game is played over 5 rounds, where you'll take 1 turn per round. So each player only gets 5 turns.

## Big concepts

- Center of the game board is where we'll build pyramids
- To the left we have a section where scoring tiles will go
- To the right is the sacrifice track
- Up top are the trade tiles that will give us bonuses and abilities
- Surrounding the pyramid are three different areas where we'll be constructing buildings
- There is a place beneath the pyramids where an action card will show what buildings score this round

Three basic types of resources: Square 
- wood, brick, and stone
- used to expand presence on board and build economy

Three advanced types of resources: Round 
- corn, gold, and priests
- gain trade tiles, go up on sacrifice track, and claim rituals

## Turn structure

The game is played over 5 rounds. In each round we will all select a card to play simultaneously and put it face down in front of it

### Cards

- Cards show a resource up top. This indicates a row or column you can activate on your playerboard for income.
- Cards show a property in the center that dictates where you can build a building that turn
    - One of the three building types (fields, temples or villages)
    - One of the 3 regions (blue, yellow, or green)
    - One of the 3 terrain types (plains, mountains, or forests)
- Number on the bottom for turn order. Lowest number acts first.

The rest of your turn is all steps shown on your player aid

### Income

Look at the basic resource on the card you played. There is a row and a column on your playerboard that cooresponds to that resource. You can activate either or

Gain the basic resource and then every resource printed on a tile in that row

### Capital actions

You can perform any number of captial actions on your turn!

- Spend gold to get a trade tile. 
    - You can only buy one tile from each row on your turn
    - 1/2/3 coins depending on row
    - Row 1 provide a 1 time benefit
    - Row 2 provide a special ability you can do once per round that is available immediately
    - Row 3 provide more powerful once per round abilities and permanent passive effects
    - Tiles are refreshed after the active player's turn
- Build pyramid
    - Spend 1 priest and one of each basic resource to build a level of a pyramid
    - Must be built in an empty pyramid space on the board, or one with an incomplete pyramid
    - If you build on an empty space, grab a scoring tile and put it next to the pyramid space. Then place the largest pyramid piece you have available in that spce
    - If you contribute to an existing pyrmaid, the piece you place must be smaller and go on top of the existing piece. 
    - A player can only build one level of the same pyramid each round, but may contribute to multiple pyramids in the same round
    - A pyramid is complete when no more pieces can go on top of it
        - So a small pyramid is complete with only 1 piece, but that can only happen if a player has already build of of their large and medium pieces
        - A medium pyramid is a medium size piece and a small piece
        - A large pyramid is one piece of each size
    - At game end pyramids award points for buildings matching that scoring tile
- Ritual action
    - You must have built at least one pyramid level to do a ritual
    - Spend a priest and place a disk on one of the ritual cards on display
        - You can only have one disk on a ritual card
        - You can place a disk on cards with other players disks, but must pay 1 gold to the supply for each piece already there
        - The number of ritual cards you can have a disk on is limited by the number of total pyramid pieces you have build (so to place out all three disks, you must have built 3 pyramid pieces)
    - At game end, players get points for ritual cards with their disks on them
- Sacrifice action
    - Spend one priest and up to 5 corn
    - Advance on the sacrifice track equal to corn spent
    - Gain the bonus from every space you pass over and end on
    - Can share spaces, just put your piece on top of other players
    - Third step offers discount of 1 gold on level 2 trade tiles
    - Sixth step offers discount of 2 gold on level 3 trade tiles
    - 9th step allows yhou to place on ritual cards without spending a priest
    - 11th step allows you to place a disk on a ritual card at no cost independent of pyramid levels that you've built

After performing all the captial actions you want, you do the construction step

### Construction

You may spend basic resources to build any number of buildings

- playerboard shows you the cost of each building type up top, and the palace on the far right, which we'll get to
- You can only build in a spot with a building tile
- The space must be allowed by your action card
    - If your card depicts a building type, you can only build that building type but any region or terrain type
    - If your card depicts a region or terrain type you can build any building types available in that region or terrain type
- It is possible for there to be no legal placements
- When you build a house, replace the tile on the board where you build and put it wherever you want on your playerboard
- If the tile you place covers up a symbol, gain that bonus

Building the palace

- Once per game you can build your palace
- Can only be built where your action card allows
- Take the building tile of the board and put it face down on your palace space, not the resource grid
- Put your palace and a house from your board onto the space on the main board
- The tile put on your board will not produce, but will count as two houses for end game scoring purposes

### Scoring

Score 2 poitns for each house you have on the board that matches the property in the center of the action card (so score all of a building type, all buildings in a region, or all buildings in a terrain type)

Draw a card from the face up action cards to end your turn, do not refill

## End of round

Replace the scoring card with the last card left in the action card lineup

Collect the action cards played this round, add the top card from the deck, and use these to make the new action card offer

## End of game

The game ends after the 5th round

- Sacrifice track
    - Top players gain 9/6/3 points (ties broken by who is on bottom of stack)
    - Must have moved to get poitns
- Rituals
    - Score ritual cards where you have a disk
- Pyramids
    - Each pyramid has a scoring tile
    - Players that contributed to a pyramid get points for each of their houses matching the tile
    - Each pyramid piece awards 1 point per matching house (so if you have 2 pieces in the pyrmaid you get 2 points per house)
    - If the pyramid is complete, each piece awards its owner a bonus 5 points
