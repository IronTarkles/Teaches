## Overview

The planet is dying and we're migrating our societies to a new planet!

The game is played over 4 rounds

## Board overview

THe gross yellow planet is the old world where all of our existing stuff is, the other planet is the new world ripe for expansion.

- Round track up top
- Turn order in corner
- Sattelite track twisting between both planets
- Market of statdiums, spaceships, and 

- Playerboard
    - Center of the board is technologies
    - Section at the bottom of the board represents your actions
    - Your color player disks are population markers
    - Left side has a production track for the new world, with a marker for each resource

!!! 
    
    Important concept: old world vs new world 

    The two sides of your playerboard represent the old world and the new world. Each of these has to have its components and production kept separate. Resources and cards built on the old world side need to be kept on the old world side, and vice versa. Spaceships can (and will) be used to transport things from the old world to the new world. As you get set up on the new world, more and more of your resources will be produced over on that side of your board.

    If an effect tells you to gain something, you choose which world it comes in on

## Round overview

Each round is made up of 7 phases

### Income

Produce resources on both worlds, feed and entertain populations

- Each of your worlds produces
    - Old world
        - Each icon will produce the resource of it's color. At the start of the game, this is 7 of each resource
    - New world
        - Produce based on resource markers on production track. At the start of the game, this is 0 for everything
- Feed populations
    - Refer to table near the round tracker on the main board
    - Shows how much food is needed for each world in each round
    - So in round 1, you need 5 food for the old, nothing for the new
    - Where in round 4, you'll need 1 food for old, and 4 for the new
    - If you can't pay the full cost, take 1 penalty chip for every food you can't pay
- Entertainment
    - Refer to the table near the round tracker on the main board
    - Check if the new world has enough stadiums
    - At the start of the 3rd round you must have at least 1, start of the 4th round you must have 2, end of the game you must have 3
    - For each stadium you're missinga at one of these checks, take a penalty chip

### Actions

We will take turns taking a single action in turn order. There are ten icons at the bottom of your board that show the possible actions. The number under each action block represents its power

- Choose an action to perform
- Play a card
    - You can play a card face down under the block with the action you want to perform, and will then perform that action
    - You can also play a card to perform it's text effect instead of an action printed on the board
- Advance your action marker one space on the track (the orange one above the card slots) and then pay any energy cost shown. 
    - Your first two actions are free, with further actions costing you energy
    - You will pay an additional energy for playing the 4th (and further) card in a single power slot (reminder shown next to action track)
    - The power slot on the far right of the board can only have a single card played under it
- Where energy paid for actions comes from depends on your markers position on the progress track (reminder in yellow box on halfway point of progress track)
    - If both of your markers are before the halfway point, then the action energy cost is paid from the old world
    - If you have one marker on either side of the halfway point, then energy can be paid from either or both worlds
    - If both markers are beyond halfway, energy cost is paid from the new world

#### World agnostic actions

None of these actions are associated with a particular world

- Gain and replace (left side of 4)
    - Gain one of any resource to either world (symbol is the three way box)
    - Up to three times you may exchange one resource for any other resource (on the same world)
    - You can peform these exchanges split up between the planets, but the exchanges are within each planet (can't remove from A to place on B)
- Research a tech (right side of 4 and 1)
    - You have 3 columns of techs on your playerboard, with research happening in each column bottom to top
    - Taking the resource actions lets you place a marker on a tech
    - The level 1 techs require only 1 research to activate
    - Level 2 and 3 require two research actions. For the first step put your marker on the left, the second step move it to the right
    - If there is a lightning bolt, resolve the effect as soon as the tech is completed. The effect is the text, the icon up top just shows which action or phase the tech is associated with

#### Either world actions

These actions require you spending resources to build things, which can be done on either of the two worlds

- Build spaceship (middle of two)
    - At the start of this action, you can spend 1 resource from any planet to discard any number of ships from the lineup, slide remaining ships down, and replenish (max once)
    - Choose any ship from the market. Slide and replenish immediately
    - Pay the resource cost shown in the top left (cost must come totally from one world), then the ship is built into that world (so on that side of your board)
- Build a stadium (right of two)
    - Works exactly the same way, including optional refresh
- Build a factory (left of 3)
    - Spend a resource correspoinding to a factory tile that you still have
    - Build that type of factory onto the world where you paid the resources

The things built with these actions can all be built on either world, and can be transported between worlds

#### New world actions

- Cloning (middle of 3)
    - Pay one food (green) to produce two population markers (player color disks) into the new world (on your board)
- Settling (left of 2)
    - Choose an empty valid hex on the new world
    - To be valid, must meet these conditions:
        - Hex must be in a biome that you are allowed to settle in. Starting off you can only build in white. One one of your progress markers has advanced far enough the yellow symbol means you can build in desert, then the green symbol means you can build in forests, blue means water
        - If there are settlement icons at the top of your hex in the black banner (suns and hurricane swirls), then you must currently have those icons available based on the position of your markers on the progress track. Your marker must currently be in a space with that icon to be able to build in a space with that icon requirement.
        - You must have enough population and/or factories to completely settle the hex. Population markers go on circles, factories go on squares. So a spot with two cirlces and a square requires two population markers and one factory. Colors are not a requirement, they are a bonus.
    - Pay 1 steel for each population marker settled (free for each factory)
    - Place all the pieces on the board, and gain one production for each piece placed (each color factory placed, and each color population marker covered up). Production is tracked by a separate disk for each resource on the new world produciton track.
    - The stronger biomes have markers on population spots that indicate multiple production level gains
- Infrastructure (right of 3, left of 1)
    - Each building icon lets you take 1 infrastructure action
    - An infrastructure action is taking a card or paying the resources to play an infrastructure card to the new world
    - Like with other markets, you have an optional refresh
    - Each infrastructure card is a tile placement object you'll be trying to complete to get the production bonuses shown on the card
        - Some of these are as simple as have a number of settled spaces in specified terrains
        - Some are as complicated as have settled specific terrains in a straight line
    - An infrastructure card can be played regardless if you currently meet the condidtion or not. Once it's in play you gain the rewards immediately whenever you fufill the conditions on a later turn (or if you meet it now). You'll gain the new world production shown on the card.
    - You can only gain the bonus of a card once, and you cant' build multiple cards with the same illustration (an exclaimation mark up top indicates duplicates in the deck)

#### Passing

- If you can't afford to take another action, or just don't want to, you must pass. 
- Move your turn order marker down and out of the line up. Your turn will be skipped the rest of the action phase. 
- Once all players have passed, we'll move on to the transport phase

### Transport

How you use your spaceships to transport resources and buildings to the new world. Empty ships will return from the new world to the old. 

- You may transport with any number of old world ships. For each ship used, pay the energy cost to fuel it shown on the left
- Evacuation bay (the half cirlces)
    - For each half circle on the card (in between cost and capacity) you may evacuate a matching colored old world site
    - Choose an old world site (circle token) showing the same color as the half circle indicates
    - If it's population, you would flip over the token on the old world and put the indicated number of population markers onto the ship
    - if its a factory, flip over the token on the old world and put a matching factory from your supply onto the ship
    - A factory put in your ship using the evacuation bay icon does not count against the factory capacity of the ship
- Ship capacity
    - You can also transport factories, stadiums, and resource cubes matching the capcity shown in the top right
    - You can load a factory as part of the building capacity and not use an evacuation bay for it (this is the only way to remove factories that don't have an evac bay color on the site)

- You may also transport empty ships from the new world back to the old world
    - This is a cost of 1 energy per ship
    - They can never hold any goods, will always be empty

- Once all decisions have been made, move ships to their destinations
    - All energy paid for fuel costs are returned to the supply
    - All stuff brought to the new world is moved out of the ships onto that side of the board

### Turn order

- Add up happiness (smiley face icons) from new world, old world, and tech track
- Re-arrange players on turn order track in order from happiest to least happy
- If tied, swap relative order

### Progress

- Add up total power of all actions taken that round (number in action slot and on cards played for effect)
- In new turn order, each player will move their progress markers a combined number of spaces equal to that power
- Only green spaces count. The black spaces are bonuses whenever you pass them, but don't count as a space on the track
- You can move your markers at different paces, but can never have more than one transition space inbetwen your two markers
- You will initially move along the "passing lane" which is the black stripe. If your marker ends its movement on a segment where there are circle icons present in the green bands of the track, you can move your marker to one of those circles to gain the rewards.
- Only a single player may occupy each of these bonus spaces.
- All future progress movement phases will begin by moving all tokens back to the passing lane. Meaning whoever goes first will have full choice of any bonuses, even in previously full spots
- At two players, only the inner lane is available for placement
- Passing the first black "bonus" space means you flip over one of those tokens on the old world, losing that produciton
- When your second marker passes the halfway point, you'll reclaim one of those tokens onto the new world side of your playerboard and increase each of your productions by one. The token does not need to be settled to gain this effect.
- You'll gain the second token whenever both your progress markers have crossed the forest build space. 

### Bonus check

Friendly bonus, get the lower one no matter what, get the higher one if you meet it
