## General

We play strangers venturing into the legendary city-state of Naqala to maneuver the local tribes (spoilers, there's five of them), invoke Djinns and gain influence. At the end of the game the player with the most points is crowned Sultan and wins

All money is private in this game.

## Round Structure

### Bidding for Turn order

We'll start each round by bidding for the order that we'll take turns in.

The player whose marker is in the 1 spot on the bid order track will move their marker to a space on the turn order track, paying the price shown.

If you bid zero, you're placed in the bold zero spot. Anyone that bids zero after you will cause your piece to bump down into a lower zero spot.

### Player actions

In this turn order we'll do the following actions on our turn

- Move turn order marker off of the turn track and put it on the first empty spot on the bid order track

#### Move meeples

Select a tile that has at least one meeple. Take all the meeples from that tile and drop them onto adjacent tiles one at a time, following these rules:

- The last meeple you place must be on a tile with at least 1 meeple of the same color
- You can't place meeples onto diagonal spaces
- You can't immediately back track, but can circle back around

You can pass through empty tiles placing one meeple there as you move across, but you may not end your meeple placement on an empty tile

Once finished placing meeples, take the last meeple you placed on the final tile back into your hand, along with all matching meeples of that color from that space.

If removing all the meeples of this color leaves the tile empty, you place a camel onto the space to show that you control it. This will get you points at the end of the game

#### Tribes actions

All the meeples are from different tribes that do different actions. Depending on the color meeples you grabbed from the last tile, you'll do a different action

- Yellow
    - Place in front of you, worth VP at end of game
- White
    - Place in front of you, worth VP at end of game
    - Can also be spent to gain Djinn and use their powers
- Green
    - Place back in bag
    - Grab a number of resource cards from the far end of the line equal to the number of meeples
    - Don't replace cards
- Blue
    - Place back in bag
    - Look for blue point tiles surrounding yours up, down, left, right and diagonal (counting your tile should be 9)
    - Take the number of blue point tiles times the number of meeples you picked up and gain that much gold
    - May discard Fakir cards from your hand to add +1 to the multiplyer
- Red
    - Place back in bag
    - Kill one meeple within range, removing it from its tile and putting it in the bag
    - Range is up to a number of tiles away equal to the number of meeples (can't count diagonal)
    - OR kill a yellow or white meeple in front of another player, putting it back in bag
    - May discard Fakir cards to increase range by +1 each
    - Killing the last meeple on a tile claims it for you, so you put a camel on it

#### Tile action

Once you've distributed meeples and taken the tribe action, you'll do the tile action

- Perform the action of the tile you placed your last meeple on. TIles with a red arrow are a MUST, others are MAY
- Oasis: Place a plam tree on the tile, it is now worth more end game points. No limit to number of trees on a single tile (components are limited)
- Palace: Place a palace tile, it is now worth more end game points. No limit to number of palaces except components
- Cards: Pay the money indicated, then take 1 card from the first 3 cards of the line up, or 2 cards from the first 6 cards of the line up. Don't replace cards.
- Djinn: Pay either 2 white meeples or 1 meeple and a Fakir to take a Djinn card of your choice from the line up
    - Don't replace yet
    - Djinn give you end game points
    - Some have special powers for free or a cost. Ones with a cost can only be used once per turn
    - You can use a power the turn you get a Djinn (as long as you can pay it)

#### Sell goods (optional)

As the last action on your turn you may sell goods (but not Fakir)

The number of unique cards in the set determines how much money you get.

You may sell as many sets as you want.

### Clean up

Once all players have taken a turn, refill all Djinn and goods. Before refilling goods, slide them all up to the front of the row and then draw up to 9

Now we bid for turn order for the next round

### End of the game

Two ways for the game to end:

- A player drops their last camel
- There are no legal Meeple movements possible

### Scoring

- 1 VP per gold meeple
- 1 VP per yellow meeple
    - +10 points for each opponent with less than you
- 2 VP per white meeple
- Sum of all VP on your Djinn
- 3 VP for each palm tree on tiles with your camels
- 5 VP for each palace on tiles with your cames
- Sum of all VP on tiles with your camels
- Sum of VP for goods left in hand
