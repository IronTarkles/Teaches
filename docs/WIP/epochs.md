## Overview

In this big ol civ game, we're all different civilizations trying to get the most victory points over 3 epochs to show that we're the best leaders

The game is played over 3 rounds, or epochs, representing ancient, medieval, and modern times.

Each epoch will have a brief setup, player turns, and then a brief ending phase

(Just read the overview from the rulebook for flavor)

## Turn structure

Each players turn will consist of an upkeep phase, an action phase, and a military phase

### Upkeep

- Draw an invention card of the next epoch into hand
    - This isn't done in the third epoch since there are no cards of the next epoch
- Add the top card of the market deck to the market (the horizontal cards w/ the current epoch on them)
    - Card will be placed in the first available spot in it's appropriate row on the board
    - If no room, last card in row is discarded and everything slides to make room for the new card
- Reveal one of the face down map tiles (if there are any left)
    - Put any pieces on the terrain if there are icons
    - You will need to connect the highest priority terrain that you are able to. So connect seas, if no seas or unable to, plains, etc

### Action phase

You have 2 choices in your action phase. Either invent and perform 1 action, or perform 2 actions but do not invent

#### Invent

- Play a current epoch card from your hand or the market to the area above your board
    - To play a card from the market, you must replace it with a card from your hand (of the current epoch)
    - Abilities on cards you invent will persist between epochs
    - You can't have 2 of the same invention
- Gain bonuses from the invention card
    - Top left debut bonus is earned immediately by you, and the player to your left
    - A bonus with an exclamation point is gained immediately
    - A bonus with an hourglass is endgame
    - All other abilities are passives

Some of the bonuses you can gain from cards include:

- Gaining resources
    - Whenever you gain resources, they are stored in your hourglass
    - Basic resources up top, gold on bottom.
    - Gold can act as a wild resource for the 3 basic resources. 
- Components
    - You have a supply of components that are not available for use
    - Whenever you gain any of these components, you put them on your playerboard
    - Components on your playerboard are availabe for use
- Culture
    - Whenever you see the purple culture symbol, go up on the culture track by that nuymber
    - Can share spaces, stack on top. Top is considered higher

Before moving on to actions, a note about inventions

- You MUST invent 3 cards in an epoch
- So if you get down to the last two turns of an epoch and have only invented once, you must take your turns as invent plus one action
- You can't sequence things so that you take actions that would leave you unable to have invented 3 times in an epoch
