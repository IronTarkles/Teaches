    Darwin's Journey

    Overview

    This is a worker placement game where we're recalling Charles Darwin's journey through the Galapagos Islands, where he developed his theory of evolution. The game is played over 5 rouds, with a specific objective every round that will award victory points. At the end of 5 rounds, the player with the most victory points is the winner.

    Key Concepts

    • You can use things gained from one action to pay costs of following actions, you don't need to have everything up front
    • Objective tiles and crew cards can be claimed at any point during the action or reward phases, this could result in a player taking actions outside of the actions phase

    Round structure

    In turn order each player will place one worker and carry out associated actions. We will continue like this until no one has workers left to place. At this point we will move on to the reward phase which we'll talk about more later

    Worker placement

    Before we dive into anything else, lets talk about how worker placement works.

    Diaries

    The board is broken up into several diaries. If a red bookmark token runs through a diary, then that diary is considered to have 2 different sections. If there is no token, the entire diary is considered on section. This matters because placing a worker into a diary section that contains another worker of any color (including your own) incurs a penalty of paying additonal coins. At 2 players it costs 3 coins, at 3-4 players it costs 2 coins.

    Locations

    There are 3 differently shaped worker placement locations.

    • Circular magnifying glass
    • example: at the top of the main actions

    • Any number of workers can go here, but you must pay the penalty cost if it is not the first worker in that notebook seciton

    • Unlocked Lenses

    • Example: the lenses placed on the non-standard actions during setup
    • Lens actions can be printed on the main board, unlocked with neutral lenses during setup, or unlocked during the game with player color lenses
    • Only one worker may go here, and you must pay the penalty cost if it is not the first worker in that notebook section

    • If you use a lense that is a different player's color, that player gains one coin from the supply. This does not work on your own color lenses.

    • Square or rectangular magnifying glass

    • Example: turn in specimen
    • Any number of workers may be placed here without paying any penalty

    Wax Seal Requirements

    • In this game, actions require your worker to have specific seals in order to be able to take that action
    • On your playerboard, most worker has a track associated with it with empty spaces for wax seals
    • Your topmost worker has a purple seal with no empty spaces for additional seals. This purple seal counts as wild and can count as any one seal when taking an action.
    • There are also one time use purple seal tokens you can use when placing a worker that can count as any one seal.
    • A worker can never go to an action space unless they have at least the required amount of seals depicted on the space

    Action Spaces

    Red seal spot

    This action allows you to purchase new seals for your workers. For each symbol you may purchase one seal from the offer. Each row of the offer shows the cost associated with it. When you purchase a seal you put it in any row on your playerboard. Placed seals are permanent. Some seals have a placement cost associated with them. Once you've placed enough seals on a worker, that worker can have a silver or gold distinction. Many actions get you additional benefits if the worker used in that space has a distinction. This action space for example gets you a second seal purchase with a silver distinction, and a discount of 1 on the entire action with a gold distinction. There are addtional spaces here that require more seals, but these will need player lenses to unlock. More about that later.

    Blue seal spot

    This action allows you to move your ship along the map a number of spaces up to that shown on the top right of the icon. The individual ship spaces on the map can hold any number of different ships. You gain the bonus of any space where your ship ends it's movement. Some spaces will get you bonuses like allowing you to place out tents. You pay any associated cost and remove the topmost tent from your playerboard, gaining any bonus shown next to that space. If you land on a specimen you place a clear marker on your playerboard to show that you have obtained it. More on what to do with those later. When a player's ship passes a silver banner with a meeple, that player puts an explorer meeple on the next island's starting space. Unlockable spots here give additional movement, and one of the distinction allows you to grab the benefit of a space you pass over as well as the one you end up on.

    Green seal spot

    This action allows you to move your explorer meeples on the different islands on the main board. Pick an island with an explorer and move it up to the amount of spaces shown on the action icon. Unlike the boat spaces, only one meeple can be present on an island space (except for the starting space of an island). You skip over any occupied spaces without using movement points. You earn the bonus of where your worker ends up. If you are the first to pass a banner, you gain the reward shown. Similarly to the blue action, one of the distinctions here lets you grab an additional reward you pass over.\

    Yellow seal spot

    This action allows you to move stamps from your playerboard onto an envelope of the main board. The icon shows how many stamps you may place. You may take stamps from mulitple stacks on your playerboard, but you have to place them all on the same envelope. Whenever you empty all the stamps from a stack, you gain the bonus shown on your playerboard. Each envelope you put your stamps on awards a bonus at the end of the round. More on that later

    Unlock lens spot

    Allows you to unlock stronger actions by putting one of your lens tokens on a space. Every locked lens space shows a cost that must be paid to place a lens toke n there. When you place a lens token on a space, you then take the action that space shows without needing to use a worker or meet any seal requirements. For the rest of the game, any player can now place a worker on this space as long as they meet the seal requirements. If another player uses your lens token, you get 1 coin from the supply.

    Deliver specimen

    This is how you use specimens that you have collected. You can only turn in a specimen that you have a token for on your board, and you can only turn it in if it is not present in the display already. A specemin will be in the display either through set up or if turned in by another player. You gain a number of coins based on empty spaces in the row and column where your specimen was placed, and a number of moves on the theory of evolution track based on filled spaces. The first specemin in a row gets you a bonus coin, and the last specemin in a row gets a bonus movement on the theory of evolution track. You can pay 3 coins to then deliver a 2nd specemin. This action is a great way to get money, and to get more end game points. The theory of evolution track gives you a socring multiplier for end game points that we'll go over later.

    Research specemin

    Pick a tile already on the board and gain the research marker on your player board. Since you can only do this for a specemin already in the display, you can not later turn this in with the deliver specemin action. You might do this if you need a particluar color or specemin type for an objective tile.

    Gain objective tile

    Take an objective tile from the display and place it in your reserve next to your board. You can only have 2 tiles in your reserve, any further objective tiles grabbed must be placed onto an objective space on their board and they will not be able to move it later. When you complete an objective in your reserve, you can place it in the corresponding color space on your board and gain the reward (either an ability or endgame points, sometimes both). Objectives will not be refilled until the end of the round.

    Turn order

    Take some money and then move your turn order marker. Turn order will not change until the next round.

    Reward phase

    After the action phase is complete, we will move on to the reward phase after adjusting turn order for the new round.

    We will evaluate correspondence majorities, or the stamps on envelopes. We do this starting with the top envelope and moving down. The player with the majority gains the top benefit. The player in 2nd place gains the smaller one. Any tied players gain the same benefit in turn order. Any players who got a bonus will then remove half their stamps rounded up and put them in the stamp trash on the board.

    We will then score the current round's objective. If a player's ship token is behind the HMS Beagle, they will incur a lagging penalty. Each victory point total between that player's ship and the Beagle are totaled up and the player's round score is reduced by that many points. You can't go into the negative.

    Cleanup phase

    • Beagle moves to the next position
    • All workers are returned to players boards
    • Any remaining objectives are wiped and new ones are put out
    • Wax seals slide up and are then refilled

    Final scoring

    • Points for completed objectives
    • Points for workers w/ enough seals
    • Theory of evolution scoring
      • We calculate the museum modifier. It is 2 plus the number of complete rows in the musuem display. The resulting number is the musuem modifier. You then score points equal to the highest multiplier you have passed on the theory of evolution track times the museum modifier.
