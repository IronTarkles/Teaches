    Beer Pioneer

    Overview

    We're brewmasters in 1850 around the industrialization period in Germany brewing more and more beer to become the best brewer.

    Round structure

    This is a worker placement game with multi use cards played over a variable number of rounds. We'll be working on fufilling recipes to get points. On a player's turn they'll take a single action, and then the next player will take their turn. We'll continue like this until everyone has passed. The game ends after the round in which at least one player has scored at least 20 points.

    Board overview

    • The main board contains:
      • A market of cards, with our brewery level directly below
      • Some shared objectives at the top right
      • A turn order track with some extra workers that we can unlock
      • A bunch of worker placement spots to take actions
    • Side board contains:
      • Bonus action area
      • End of round stuff on the right
    • Player boards:
      • Where we'll be brewing and barreling beer and getting better at brewing
      • You start only able to brew a style of beer called alt, the rest are locked for the time being
      • Everyone has 5 workers
        • One of your workers lives near the round track, and will only go on spaces up there
        • 2 numbered "experienced" workers in your supply. You'll start with a level 1 and a level 2. These are used to take actions on the main board in spaces that show a number and a worker.
        • A truck that is used for taking specific actions at the bottom of the game board.
        • An administrative worker that lives on your playerboard
        • 2 little guys that will only be used to take bonus action on the side game board when specific conditions are met.

    Turn overview

    Everyone will take a minimum of 5 actions in a round. You can't pass if you have a worker that you can use.

    The player colored beer barrels at the top of the board indicate turn order.

    The first player will use one of their five workers, do a bonus action if applicable, then the next player will take their turn. We'll keep doing this until everyone is out of workers.

    • Each action space on the board only provides action spaces for 2 workers, one in the top and one in the bottom. We block each other from going to all spots except the one in the bottom right.
    • Only the trucks can go on truck spaces
    • Only the experienced workers (numbered) can go on the numbered worker spaces
      • If the number on the space plus the number on your worker is 6 or greater, you'll get to take a bonus action. more on that later

    If cards are drawn from the market they will refill at the end of a player's turn.

    Experienced Worker Actions

    1 - Brewing beer

    This is how you brew beer.

    • Your beer bottle at the top of the board indicates your brewing level. This is how many brewing dials you can place on your board with a single action.
    • Initally, we'll only have the ability to brew one type of beer, Alt. Later on we can expand our brewery and remove these blocker tiles and pieces to get the ability to brew new types of beer.
    • When you take this action, you'll place a number of brewing dials into your brewery equal to your brewing level
      • The black mark on a dial indicates how long it will take to brew
      • You'll set the dial to the default value at the top of the beer. For alt it is 3, but not every beer is 3.
      • You'll add the value you're currently at on your time track in the middle of your player board (the cube).
      • So if you brewed beer on your first turn you'd only be able to make alt, and the dial would be set at 6.
    • Once you have a type of beer brewing, you can't brew that beer again until the one on your board is done brewing

    2 - Rotations

    This is how you rotate your dials

    • This action moves your dials a variable amount.
    • This amount is based on symbols on your cards in your tableau.
    • Everyone starts with a hand of cards, and a starter card in play
    • Cards are multi use and depending how you play them, they will give you different effects
    • For this action, we're looking at the total number on dial symbols on the tops of cards that we have in play.
    • This is cumulative, so if you have multiple cards in your tableau with the dial symbol, add them all up.
    • This is the toal number of dial cranks you get, and you can freely split it across different barrels that you have cooking.

    If you ever move a beer dial to zero, it is ready to barrel. You'll grab that beer token from the supply.

    • It will go on an empty spot on the right side of your playerboard in the cellar.
    • Some of these spaces are currently blocked with tiles that we can remove during the game.
    • Where it goes in the cellar doesn't matter.
    • Remove the dial from your board, it is now ready to be brewed again.
    • If you don't have room in your cellar, the dial still gets removed, but you don't get to place a tile (what a waste)
    • You can always replace a beer tile or barrel in your cellar to lose instead of the newly earned one.

    3 - Improve brewing / remove blocking

    There are 3 options for this action

    • Improve your brewery progress: Move the cube on your progress track (brew time). Passing a lightning bolt gets you the bonus.
      • There is an income, a hand size bump, and a point for instant bonus
    • Remove a blocking token from your board. This can be from beer types or from your administration area where your playerboard worker is.
    • Pay 1 coin to do both

    You can remove a blocking token from an area of the brewery that hasn't been revealed yet.

    4 - Taking X barrels and or moving X barrels

    X is equal to the corresponding icons on the top of cards in your tableau.

    There are 3 options for this action:

    • Take X new barrels from the common supply and put them in your barrel depot (the top 3 barrel spaces on your board)
    • Move X barrels from your barrel depot to the cellar (hex area)
    • Pay 1 coin to do both.
    • You'll need to use empty barrels from your cellar to fufill orders, and thats how you get points!

    You can do these actions in either order, but must fully resolve one before starting the other.

    If you have upgraded your barrel depot, you'll always have 1 "virtual" barrel. When moving barrels to the cellar, you'll always take 1 from the supply as well if you have room for it

    5 - Play a card for white ability

    Play a card for the white effect. You can either:

    • Use the text at the bottom of the card and then discard it
      • Grey blocked off portrait
      • One time use effect, then discard.
    • Play it as a master brewer to the bottom cuttout of your board
      • White portrait, passive bonus
      • You can only have 1 card in this slot
      • You can replace it with a new card by taking this action

    6 - Upgrade a worker

    Pay one coin to upgrade a worker at the end of the round.

    • Pay 1 coin to take the worker upgrade tile off of your board (small square token with a worker and an up arrow) and put it on the end of round board on space 3.
    • Multiple people's tiles can be on this spot of the end of round board.
    • You will upgrade one of your two experienced workers to the next level at the end of the round.

    7 - Get a board extension

    Pay 4 coins to add an extension tile to your playerboard's right side. You have room for 4 of these.

    • The top tile of each stack gets you a point when obtained
    • Tile effects:
      • Beer filter: Remove one of the two overlay tiles blocking beer types. Also you get up to 3 rotations immediately
      • Truck fleet: Take a barrel depot with 5 storage spaces and 1 coin from the supply. This tile covers the one on the player board, and any barrels transfer to the new tile. Also provides one "virtual" barrel always available to move to the cellar
      • Cooling system: Remove one overlay tile. Also increase brewing level by one.
      • Laboratory: Remove one of your small workers from the game by putting it on step one of the round end board. From now on at the end of the round they can rotate each of their beer dials once. Also immediately get 2 dial rotations
      • Malt factory: Take a beer tile from the supply and put in into the cellar. This can let you take a type of beer that you can't brew. Additionally, you may move one barrel.
      • Machine hall: Get two additional small workers from the supply
    • When you grab an extension tile, the first one goes in the top slot of your playerboard. Subsequent extension tiles get you a bonus when placed
      • Play a card for the top effect
      • Draw a card (from brewing level range or top deck)
      • Increase brewing level
      • Increase brewing level or get a point

    8 - Get money

    Nice and simple, just get 2 coins

    Truck actions

    These actions you take with your truck worker

    • 9 space is single person, just get 2 dollars
    • Uncontested spaces:
      • Make a delivery (top action spot here also lets you move a barrel to cellar)
      • Chose one of the 3 options (put barrel in depot, move barrel to cellar, draw a card). Top action spot also gets 1 coin
      • Last space
        • Experience worker or truck can go here
        • Pay the cost up top to get the thing on bottom OR
        • Do two transactions at the cost of an additional coin

    Bonus actions

    Again, after you take an action where the experience level of your worker and the board printed value equal 6 or greater, you get to take a bonus action.

    • Take one of your two tiny workers and put them into either part of the bonus section.
    • The bottom actions are stronger, but require you to have 2 lightbulbs in your tableau
    • If you don't have any tiny workers available, you don't get a bonus action.
    • You can have multiple workers in the same spot, any amount of players can go to either as well.
    • The spots give you a choice of action
    • Top half (basic actions)
      • Take a money
      • Play a card as a green card to your tableau
      • Take X barrels to depot
      • Move X barrels from depot to cellar
      • Apply 2 rotations
      • Brew 1 beer
      • Remove 2 block token
    • Bottom half (improved actions)
      • Pay 1 coin and complete an order
      • Pay 3 coins and build an extension tile
      • Take 3 cards from the display or draw pile
      • Apply X rotations
      • Gain a victory point

    Making a delivery

    We've talked about making a delivery a couple times, what does that mean

    • Play a card from your hand and resolve the purple area
    • Return the indicated beer tiles and barrels form your own cellar
    • You will gain victory points and potentially other bonuses from doing this

    We've now talked about all of the ways to play a card

    • Geen top for adding symbols from your tableau to power X actions and give lightbulbs for stronger bonus actions
    • White lower portions for instant effects or ongoing passive bonuses
    • Purple right side deliveries for points and other rewards

    Turn order actions

    You have an untagged worker on the main board whose only purpose is to determine your turn order for the next round and get you a bonus in doing so.

    The action spaces here provide bonuses

    • Drawing cards
    • Getting coins
    • Getting temporary workers to use for the round (these are returned to the turn order track at the end of the round)
    • Playing a card to your tableau for the symbols
    • Gain a victory point

    Admin action

    This is what you do with the untagged worker locked on your playerboard

    • At the start of the game you only have one option for this worker (getting a rotation and removing a blocker tile)
    • As you remove blocker tiles, you will unlock stronger actions for this worker to take.
    • You do not have to unlock these actions in order.

    End of the round

    There are five steps that we go through for the end of the round, and we just follow along on the side board

    • Rotations
      • Get one rotation for each beer dial on your board.
      • If you have the extension tile where you sacrificed a little worker, you'll get two rotations for every beer on your board
      • These rotations are mandatory. If any hit 0, you'll remove the dial, take a beer tile from the supply, and put them in your cellar.
      • If you have no room in the cellar, the dial is still removed, and you don't get to place the beer tile (what a waste).
      • Instead of just losing it, you can chose to replace a beer tile or barrel already in your cellar, and that gets lost instead.
    • Reset pieces
      • Set new turn order based on workers there
      • All temp workers go back to turn order track
      • All players grab all their workers back
      • Admin workers on player board go to their space as well
    • Upgrade workers
      • Any player that has a tile in this space will upgrade one of their workers
      • This is upgrading an existing worker, you will never have more than 2 experienced workers of your player color
      • Replaced workers will go on your playerboard near your cellar in the marked spaces.
      • The row in which you place your worker will remove the cellar tile in that row
      • Immediately gain the effect
    • Cards
      • Discard down to 3 cards, or 6 if you've unlocked that ability on your brewing progress track on your playerboard
      • Card in the leftmost of market will fall off, slide everything down and refill.
    • Income
      • Get money if you've gone up on this track
      • Bump on the brewing progress track
      • Other bump from tile in cellar

    End of game

    When someone hits 20 points or more, that will be the final round

    • We play out the rest of the round like normal
    • We do the end of round phase like normal
    • Then go to scoring

    Scoring

    • For very lightbulb in your tableau, you may discard 1 beer tile in your cellar for a point
    • All players who have reached the end of the brewing track may pay 1 coin for each lightbulb icon in their tableau to gain one point for each
