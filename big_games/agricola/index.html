    Agricola

    Overview

    We're just simple 17th century farmers trying to make an honest living.

    We'll get points by building out our personal farms, and at the end of the game the person with the most points wins

    Round Structure

    The game is played over 14 rounds. Each round is made up of 4 phases

    • Preparation phase: Where we'll reveal a new action space and place out goods on spaces that accumulate them
    • Work Phase: Where we'll take turns putting a single worker on a vacant action space on the board
    • Return home phase: Where we'll take all our workers back and move on to the next round
    • Optional Harvest phase: Where we harvest crops, breed animals, and feed our families

    The boards

    The game board has a number of action spaces, with a number of empty spots where new actions will be available every round.

    At the end of certain rounds, there is a harvest (point to symbol under rounds 4,7,9, etc).

    We also have a personal board where we will build out our own farm, and a display of major improvments that we can build to our farms

    Turn Structure

    On your turn you will put one worker out onto the board. You can only put a worker on an unocupied space, which means each action space can only be used once per round.

    You must be able to perform the primary action of a space to go there.

    We start the game with only 2 workers, but will be able to get more as we grow our home and family

    Action spaces

    There are two different types of action spaces. Permanent actions that will always give the same result, and accumulation spaces which will build up resources over every round if no one has gone there.

    So the Forest space gets 3 wood this every round, which can really pile up if no one is going there to claim it

    Where as the day laborer spot will just give you a flat 2 food no matter what

    A lot of spaces are as simple as go there and get the resources. Wood, clay, reed, and food

    The first player spot allows you to grab the first player token. This doesn't change turn order until the start of the next round. This is the only way that turn order will ever change.

    It also allows you to play a minor improvement card from hand.

    improvement (orange) and occupation cards (yellow) cards can give you a variety of bonuses

    • Prerequisites in the top left
    • Cost in the top right
    • End game points on middle of card
    • Ability at bottom

    Occupation cards

    One space allows you to play an occupation casd from hand. The first one is free, each time you play another card will cost you one food.

    Occupation cards function in the same way as minor improvement cards and have the same info on them.

    You will never be able to draw new minor improvements or occupations.

    Fields and crops

    The plow field action lets you grab a field tile. You place it on your player board. If you already have a field on your board, a new one must go adjacent to it.

    You can also take an action to take 1 grain.

    The sow action allows you to plant gain or pumpkins in your fields. You must have a token to pay for this action. It gets put in your field and additional tokens get stacked on it that you will add to your personal supply during harvests

    You can plant as many tokens in a single action as you want (must have tokens to spend and free room to plant them)

    You can also use grain to bake bread if you have an oven (more on that later)

    Building rooms

    This action space allows you to build an extra room onto your house. Extra rooms are required to get more workers later on, as you can only house 1 worker per room

    Everyone starts the game with wood houses, and there is an action later on that allows you to renovate your house to clay or stone

    Your house has to be all rooms of the same type.

    This action space lets you pay the cost associated with your type of house to build an additional room adjacent to an existing one. You can build as may rooms as you can pay for.

    Stables and fences

    This action space also lets you build a stable, which may go on any empty space on your board. Each space can only have one stable.

    Stables can be put in fenced in pastures.

    An un fenced stable can hold one animal.

    This is a good time to talk about the additional action deck. The game is split into stages. The same 4 action cards will always show up in stage one, but we don't know the order of them.

    One of the cards in stage one allows you to build fences.

    Each piece of wood you pay makes one piece of fence. Fences must be placed so that they full enclose one or more spaces at the time they are built. This is a pasture.

    On later turns, fences must be built adjacent to an existing pasture.

    Pastures can share walls.

    A fenced off pasture can hold 2 animals of a single type per space in the pasture.

    A stable doubles the number of animals that a pasture can hold.

    Fences can't be moved or removed later in a game. A large pasture can have fences built in it to split into smaller pastures though.

    Animals

    Some actions allow you to grab animals. You take all the animals from the space

    Your house can store a single animal (entire house, not each room), but otherwise you must put animals into pastures you have fenced off, or stables that you have built.

    Any animals you grab that you don't have room for must be immediately cooked or they just go away.

    You can freely re-arrange animals between your pastures, stables, and house at any time.

    Improvements

    We talked about improvements already, but one of the stage one cards allows you to construct a major improvement.

    These are in a shared market, and are limited.

    They show a cost in the top right. You can use the fireplace to pay for a cooking hearth as an alternative cost.

    The main benefit of the major improvements is to give you ways to convert resources into food.

    Some of these improvements allow you to spend grain when taking an action that says "bake bread" to gain food.

    You can only bake bread when taking an action that says to, where the other conversions on the card can be done at any time.

    Harvest

    At the end of each round we'll all take our workers back, reveal a new action card, and put resources on accumulation spots. At the end of certain rounds there is a harvest.

    • Get crops from fields
      • If you have planted crops, take one token from each field and add it to your supply
    • Feed your workers
      • Pay 2 food for each worker you have
      • 1 grain or 1 veggie can convert to a single food
      • For each token you can't pay, you take a begging card that is worth -3 points at the end of the game
    • Breed animals
      • For each animal that you have 2 or more of in a pasture, you get +1 animal. This means that a pasture with 4 sheep will only breed one additional sheep.
      • You must have room for the new animal.

    Later stage actions

    Rennovation

    Convert your house to the next level up by paying one resource per room plus one reed

    Can't skip clay, must go from wood to clay, then clay to stone.

    Your house must always be of the same material, so you have to be able to rennovate the whole thing with this action

    Family growth

    If you have an empty room, you can get a new worker. The family member gets added to the action space, so they are not able to be used this round.

    If you get a new worker in a harvest round, they will only cost you 1 food.

    End game

    At the end of the game, you'll get negative points for all of your empty spaces on your player board and any begging cards that you have.

    You'll also lose points for having too few of stuff
